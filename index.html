1. Identify — Find the Right Companies

Using Clay and LinkedIn Sales Navigator, we build targeted lists of companies and decision-makers who are the ideal buyers for your services. We target Maintenance Directors, Plant Managers, VP of Operations, Reliability Directors, L&D Managers, and Training Coordinators at manufacturing plants, refineries, utilities, mining operations, pharma facilities, food processing plants, and government organizations with 500+ employees.

2. Reach — Get in Front of Decision-Makers

Through a combination of cold email sequences, LinkedIn connection requests, and LinkedIn content, we put your name, credentials, and value proposition directly in front of the people who approve training budgets. The outreach is personalized, references the prospect's industry, and leads with value — not a sales pitch.

3. Engage — Build Trust Before the Ask

Consistent LinkedIn content from your profile — stories from 35 years of experience, graphic novel previews, industry insights, and practical tips — builds familiarity and credibility with prospects before they ever receive an outreach message. The free Lubrication e-book, free monthly webinar, and free articles further establish you as a trusted resource, not just another consultant.

4. Convert — Close Training Contracts and Book Orders

Once a prospect is engaged — whether through outbound outreach, inbound inquiry, or webinar attendance — we guide them toward a no-pressure discovery call. The conversion conversation is simple: here's what you offer, here's what companies like theirs have gained from it, and here's how we can structure it for their team. The 98% satisfaction rating and Fortune 600 client list do the heavy lifting.

5. Retain — Grow the Relationship

After the first engagement, the focus shifts to maximizing long-term value. A company that books a one-day training becomes a candidate for multi-day programs. A training client becomes a consulting prospect. A consulting client becomes a recurring annual relationship. We maintain contact through email newsletters, LinkedIn engagement, and periodic check-ins — ensuring you stay top of mind when the next training budget is approved.

This system operates as a continuous loop. Identification finds new prospects, outreach initiates contact, content builds trust, conversion generates revenue, and retention increases lifetime value. Every channel we use feeds into this single, unified engine.

05 — Offers Structure

The Value Ladder

Your offerings are structured into clearly defined levels. Each offer is designed to either generate immediate revenue or move prospects deeper into the ecosystem. The beauty of this model is that every level naturally feeds into the next.

1. Free Content — Top of Funnel

The free Lubrication e-book, free articles on the website, YouTube videos, and a free monthly public webinar. These attract maintenance professionals into your world, capture their email addresses, and begin building trust. Cost to the prospect: zero. Value to us: a qualified lead.

2. Books — Low-Friction Entry Point ($18.95 each / Bundle at 34% off)

The 9-book Elements of Great Maintenance Management graphic novel series. For individuals, these are an affordable way to learn. For companies, bulk orders of 50-200 copies serve as training materials — generating $2,000-$15,000+ per order. The books also open the door to training conversations: "Now that your team has the books, would you like the author to come train them in person?"

3. Webinars & Public Training — Mid-Tier ($99-$299/person)

Premium paid webinars and virtual sessions open to individual professionals. These generate mid-tier revenue and serve as a proving ground — attendees who experience your teaching firsthand often recommend you to their organizations for in-house training.

4. Corporate In-House Training — Primary Revenue Driver ($5,000-$25,000+)

This is the anchor offering. Companies hire you to train their maintenance teams — either virtually or on-site — on topics from the book series. Sessions range from half-day workshops to multi-day intensive programs. Each engagement typically includes a bulk book order on top of the training fee, making the average deal value $8,000-$30,000. This is where the real money is, and this is what our outbound engine is built to sell.

5. Consulting — Highest Ticket ($10,000-$50,000+)

Full maintenance operations audits, CMMS selection and optimization, productivity studies, preventive maintenance program development, and change management support. These engagements come from organizations that already trust you — typically after a training engagement or a referral. Our marketing creates the conditions for these conversations to happen naturally.

The value ladder is clear: free content captures attention, books build credibility, webinars demonstrate expertise, training generates primary revenue, and consulting delivers the highest lifetime value. Every marketing dollar we spend is designed to move prospects up this ladder.

06 — LinkedIn & Outbound Strategy

The B2B Revenue Machine

Your buyers live on LinkedIn. Maintenance Directors, Plant Managers, VP of Operations, L&D Heads, Reliability Engineers — they're all there. This is our primary channel for generating corporate training contracts and consulting engagements. We run three plays simultaneously:

LinkedIn Organic — Authority Building

We will publish 3-4 posts per week from your personal LinkedIn profile. Content includes short stories from your 35 years of field experience, graphic novel page previews (these are visual scroll-stoppers in a feed full of boring industrial content), practical maintenance tips and insights, industry commentary on workforce shortages and reliability challenges, and client success stories. We ghost-write all content — you review and approve. The goal is to grow your LinkedIn following significantly within 6-8 months and establish you as the most visible maintenance thought leader on the platform.

LinkedIn Paid — Targeted Visibility

Sponsored posts and message ads targeting specific job titles: Maintenance Manager, Reliability Director, Plant Manager, VP Operations, Training Manager, L&D Director — at companies with 500+ employees in manufacturing, energy, mining, utilities, pharma, and food & beverage. The ad creative will showcase the graphic novel pages — that visual format is unlike anything else in the industrial space. Budget details are in the Investment section.

Outbound via Clay — Targeted Prospecting

This is the direct revenue engine. Using Clay for lead enrichment and LinkedIn Sales Navigator for targeting, we build highly specific prospect lists of decision-makers at the right companies. Each prospect receives a personalized outreach sequence — a custom introduction referencing their industry and role, a value proposition highlighting your credentials and unique training approach, specific relevance to their likely challenges (workforce development, reliability improvement, compliance training), and a no-pressure invitation to a brief conversation.

Volume: 200-400 targeted prospects per month. Goal: 5-10 discovery calls per month, converting to 1-3 training contracts per month.

Why This Works: The combination of LinkedIn content (which prospects will see organically) and direct outreach (which lands in their inbox) creates a surround-sound effect. When a Maintenance Director receives a personalized email about your training, and then sees your name on LinkedIn sharing a brilliant maintenance insight two days later — the credibility compounds. This is not cold outreach. This is warm, multi-touch B2B engagement.

07 — Paid Advertising Strategy

Controlled Growth, Data-Driven Scaling

Paid advertising will supplement our outbound and organic efforts by driving consistent traffic to the website, generating email captures, and building retargeting audiences. We use two platforms strategically:

LinkedIn Ads — B2B Precision

LinkedIn advertising gives us surgical precision in reaching the exact job titles and company sizes that buy corporate training. We run sponsored content ads featuring graphic novel previews and training offer highlights, and message ads for high-value prospects. This is more expensive per click than Meta, but the targeting quality for B2B is unmatched — every impression lands on a relevant decision-maker.

Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram) — Scale & Retargeting

Meta ads serve two purposes: driving the free Lubrication e-book download to build the email list at scale, and retargeting website visitors and email subscribers with book offers and webinar invitations. Meta's cost per lead is significantly lower than LinkedIn, making it ideal for top-of-funnel list building. We do not use Meta for direct B2B outreach — LinkedIn and Clay handle that.

Budget Approach

We start lean, test performance, and scale based on real data. Month 1-2 focuses on testing audiences and creatives. Month 3+ scales winning campaigns and introduces retargeting. Full budget breakdown is in the Investment section.

Retargeting Strategy

Most visitors will not convert on their first touch. We implement retargeting campaigns for website visitors who viewed the training or consultation pages, users who downloaded the free e-book but haven't purchased, webinar attendees who haven't inquired about in-house training, and LinkedIn profile visitors and ad engagers. This keeps you top-of-mind and significantly improves conversion rates over time.

08 — Email Marketing Strategy

The Conversion Engine Most People Ignore

Lead Capture Points

Every touchpoint in your ecosystem feeds into the email list: the free Lubrication e-book download (already live on the website), free monthly webinar registration, website newsletter subscription, book purchase confirmation, consulting inquiry form submission, and free article readership (once the articles section is restored).

Segmented Nurture Sequences

Not all leads are equal. We build separate email sequences based on entry point:

  • Free E-Book Downloaders → 7-email sequence over 21 days. Day 1: book delivery + welcome. Day 3: a practical maintenance tip. Day 7: invitation to the free monthly webinar. Day 10: a case study or testimonial. Day 14: introduce the book series. Day 18: corporate training offer. Day 21: direct CTA to schedule a call.
  • Webinar Attendees → 5-email sequence. Recap of what was covered, additional resources, introduction to the full book series and training programs, and a direct offer for in-house training.
  • Book Buyers → 4-email sequence. Thank you + bonus content, invitation to a webinar, introduction to bulk orders for their team, and training program offer.
  • General Subscribers → Biweekly value newsletter with maintenance insights, tips from your experience, and soft CTAs to books, webinars, and training.

Ongoing Communication

Beyond automated sequences, the email list receives: a biweekly "Maintenance Insights" newsletter with your practical wisdom and one featured book or training topic, periodic promotional emails for new book releases or special offers, and targeted outreach when new webinar dates are announced. Email is where trust compounds over time — and where the highest-value conversions happen.
